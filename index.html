VANESSA ADERS  ·  Associate MFT
Supervised by Licensed Therapist

A fellow traveler on the path of personal and collective transformation.

I am a fellow traveler on the path of personal and collective transformation, committed to holding space for the full complexity of being human.

I trust the intelligence of the body, psyche, and the unseen, honoring places of erotic aliveness, grief, longing, and spiritual inquiry as gateways to healing.

My work is less about fixing and more about tending - to the soul, the unlived life, and what is quietly trying to bloom. Grounded in trauma-informed, relational, and somatic practice, I witness with reverence, curiosity, and care.

Embodied, collaborative therapy
grounded in presence and care.

My approach is embodied and process-oriented, integrating mindfulness, expressive arts, parts work, somatic attunement, and existential inquiry shaped by your unique story and needs.

We begin where you are.

I see symptoms as signals your system is doing its best to protect you. Our time is collaborative, holding space for your story to unfold in your own timing and language, whether through words, silence, movement, art, or metaphor.

Together we'll explore new ways to feel grounded, supported, and more like yourself.

Our sessions are portals, not pathologies, to what is alive, aching, or emerging. Space is held for expansive human experience, including exploration of sexuality, intimacy, and the transformative potential of non-ordinary states of consciousness.

For those who feel the ache of the in-between.

Sensitive, spiritually curious people moving through grief, transition, trauma, and identity change - met with affirming care.

Many of my clients are seekers - people who feel deeply, think expansively, and often feel they don't quite fit in. Those awakening to their aliveness, unsettled by change, or carrying grief with nowhere to place it.

My practice is affirming of all identities, with a focus on supporting LGBTQ+, neurodivergent, highly sensitive, creative, and spiritually curious folks. Some are healing from childhood wounds. Others are deconstructing rigid beliefs and reclaiming parts of themselves that had to go underground.
